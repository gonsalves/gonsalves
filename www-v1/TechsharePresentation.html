The Case for Accessibility

Abstract

My presentation develops the social case for accessibility, by examining a modified version of social contract theory. Martha Nussbaum's Frontiers of Justice forms the basis of this section, and along with an examination of the social model of disability, explains how this relates to online accessibility in this age of online governance.

From this broad perspective, it moves into a discussion of specific techniques that can be used by website authors to ensure that web pages remain usable to all users, regardless of ability. The importance of semantic markup, carefully written ALT text, the relevance of skip links and structural headings are a few of the techniques that will be examined. Some current debates (source ordered markup, skip links, accessibility of micro-formatted data, etc) will be delved into in some depth, and audience participation will be invited.

Lastly, the need for online accessibility legislation in India, similar to Section 508 legislation in the US, will be highlighted. Comparable policies (The UK's PAS 78, Canadas Common Look and Feel Standards) and standards will also be looked at. It is hoped that the entire presentation can be used as a supporting argument for anyone working towards online accessibility legislation in India.

Rahul Gonsalves, Spring 2008.
